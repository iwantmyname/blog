10 Big Brands Using Domain Hacks For URL Shortening On Twitter

No matter what you want to say on Twitter, you only get 140 characters to say it. On one hand, it's part of the reason Twitter has been so successful--it mercilessly removes fluff from online writing. But when it comes to sharing links, 140 characters can really cramp your style.

To get around this, you can shorten them using a number of generic link-shortening platforms. But if you're ambitious, you can brand your links with Bitly using a simple domain hack, like our personal favorite, iwnt.my.

For inspiration, here are ten influential brands using custom link shortening for their Twitter links:

  • Star Wars - strw.rs
  • YouTube - youtu.be
  • Bon Appetit - bonapp.it
  • Disney - nyti.ms
  • Chobani - cho.ba
  • Rolling Stone - rol.st
  • The Verge - vrge.co
  • Virgin - virg.in
  • BBC - bbc.in
  • Pepsi - pep.si

If you're interested in doing something like this for yourself, grab the shortest relevant domain you can find and head to Bitly to set up your custom short URL.

Extra Credit: Take your social networking a step further by pairing your Bitly short link with Buffer.
