Have questions? Melanie Baker is our new support scholar

First, some math. A good portion of our business comes from good-ol' North America, which creates a huge problem for us—timely time-zone support coverage. In short, we're in NZDT (UTC+13), while New York is in EST (UTC-5). 13--(+)5=18.

So when the North American East coast is looking to get their day started around 9am, it's still 3am in Wellington. And by the time "the tireds" have worn off (around 11am), much of the North American east coast is packing up to go home.

Basically, for us to hire a "Support Scholar," we had to find an agile, patient, independent, funny, intelligent master of clearly crafting the English language—who lives in North America.

Twitter is now supporting (most of the) gTLDs.

Oh look, @twitter is supporting gTLDs now. Like this one: http://iwantmyname.domains

— iwantmyname (@iwantmyname) March 10, 2014

Aside from general recognition that the new gTLDs exist, the biggest hurdle we face is platform adoption. Or, better put, getting the big social platforms to recognize gTLDs as real domain extensions.

And I'm sure you can see the barrier we face—with the internet being as segmented and siloed as it is, these domain extensions don't stand a chance unless all the big platforms get on board. Site discovery hinges on social sharing, and without it, they practically don't exist. So it brings us great joy to say that just this past weekend, Twitter made the leap.
